Spatiotemporal Dynamics of Key Climatic Variables in Turkana County: A Raster-Based Analytical Approach

A multi-variable climate dashboard for Turkana County (2025) tracking rainfall, humidity, temperature, wind speed, cloud cover, sunshine duration, and solar energy. The project was developed using ESRI Dashboards to support drought preparedness and renewable energy planning in arid regions.

🌐 View Live Dashboard

Objectives

  • Monitor climate variability across Turkana County
  • Identify seasonal trends and extreme conditions
  • Support drought early warning systems
  • Assess solar and wind energy potential

Methodology

Study Area

Turkana County, Kenya – an arid and semi-arid region highly vulnerable to drought and climate variability.

Variables Analyzed

Variable Unit Purpose
Rainfall mm Drought monitoring
Humidity % Evapotranspiration analysis
Temperature °C Heat stress monitoring
Wind Speed m/s Wind energy potential
Cloud Cover % Solar radiation influence
Sunshine hours/day Solar exposure
Solar Energy hours/day Renewable energy potential

Tools Used

  • ESRI Dashboards – interactive dashboard development
  • ArcGIS Pro / QGIS – spatial analysis and mapping
  • Excel / Python – data processing and visualization

Preprocessing

  • Extracted zonal statistics for Turkana County
  • Computed monthly averages for all variables
  • Standardized datasets for dashboard integration

Analysis

  • Computed monthly trends for all climate variables
  • Identified wet and dry seasonal patterns
  • Analyzed relationships between rainfall, temperature, and humidity
  • Integrated results into an interactive ESRI Dashboard

Results & Findings

Seasonal Patterns

  • Wet season: March–May
  • Dry season: June–February

Climate Insights

  • High temperatures coincide with low humidity
  • Solar and wind potential highest during dry periods

Key Insight

The integration of multiple climate variables into a single dashboard enables real-time monitoring and supports data-driven decision-making for drought management and renewable energy planning.

Conclusion & Recommendations

The ESRI Dashboard provides a powerful platform for visualizing climate variability in Turkana County. The findings highlight strong potential for solar and wind energy development, while also emphasizing the need for proactive drought preparedness strategies.

Recommendations

  • Adopt dashboard-based monitoring for early warning systems
  • Invest in solar energy during high sunshine periods
  • Utilize wind data for small-scale energy projects
