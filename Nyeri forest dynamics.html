FOREST COVER DYNAMICS IN NYERI COUNTY (2019–2025):
A LAND USE AND LAND COVER-BASED ASSESSMENT

Introduction

Forests are vital for climate regulation, biodiversity conservation, and sustainable livelihoods. In Nyeri County, changes in Land Use and Land Cover (LULC) have significantly influenced forest dynamics over time. This study analyzes forest cover change between 2019 and 2025, and projects trends to 2030 using LULC data in Google Earth Engine (GEE). The aim is to assess spatial and temporal forest variations and support data-driven land management and conservation planning.

Objectives

  • To quantify changes in forest area (gain or loss) over time.
  • To assess spatial patterns of forest cover change.
  • To support data-driven decision-making for sustainable land management.

Data and Methodology

Data Source: ESA World Cover datasets (Sentinel-1 & Sentinel-2, 10 m resolution).

Years of Analysis: 2019, 2025.

Preprocessing: Clipping images to Nyeri AOI and filtering for forest class.

Analysis Steps:

  1. Extract forest pixels (Class = 10).
  2. Calculate forest area (km²) for each year.
  3. Visualize spatial distribution and quantify changes using charts.

Software Used:

Google Earth Engine (JavaScript API), ArcGIS Pro for final visualization.

2019 LULC

The In 2019, forest cover (Trees) was approximately 1,507.5 km². Other land cover types included Crops (843.8 km²), Rangeland (566.5 km²), and Built Area (453.0 km²).

2025 LULC

By 2025, forest cover (Trees) increased to approximately 1,662.3 km². This represents a net gain of ~154.8 km² over six years.

LULC Change

The The change map reveals spatial patterns of forest dynamics across Nyeri County

Critical Finding: Reforestation (241.3 km²) outpaces deforestation (91.3 km²), resulting in a net gain of ~150 km². Gains are concentrated in protected water towers, while losses occur near human settlements.

Results and Discussion

Forest cover increased between 2019 and 2025, mainly in the Aberdare Ranges and Mt. Kenya slopes.

Total forest area rose by approximately 149.94 km², showing positive reforestation and recovery trends.

Interpretation and Conclusion

Nyeri County shows a net forest increase of ~154.8 km² between 2019 and 2025, with reforestation (241.3 km²) exceeding deforestation (91.3 km²). Gains are concentrated in the Aberdare Ranges and Mt. Kenya slopes – demonstrating that protected area management is effective. However, deforestation near expanding built-up areas (Built Area +160.6 km²) requires targeted intervention.

Recommendations:

Strengthen forest protection in water towers (already working)

Create buffer zones around expanding settlements

Repeat LULC analysis every 2–3 years to track trends
